The DAO Incident: The Hack That Split Ethereum

The Rise and Fall

On June 17th, 2016, the crypto world witnessed one of its most pivotal moments. The DAO, a decentralized autonomous organization that had raised an unprecedented 150 million USD in ETH, was exploited through a recursive calling vulnerability.

Quick Facts

  • Funds Raised: 11.5M ETH (~150M USD at the time)
  • Exploit Date: June 17th, 2016
  • Stolen Amount: 3.6M ETH
  • Fork Block: #1,920,000

The Aftermath

The incident led to the historic Ethereum hard fork, splitting the chain into ETH (forked) and ETC (original). This decision remains one of the most controversial moments in cryptocurrency history, raising fundamental questions about immutability and governance.

Timeline of Events

  • → April 30, 2016: DAO Launch
  • → June 17, 2016: Exploit Discovered
  • → July 20, 2016: Hard Fork Executed

Legacy

The DAO incident fundamentally changed how the crypto community approaches smart contract security and governance. Its impact continues to influence modern DeFi protocol design and security practices.
